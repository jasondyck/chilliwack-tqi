Analysis Narrative

Chilliwack scores 3.3 out of 100, placing it in the "Minimal Transit" category on the Walk Score Transit Score scale (0–24). Per the Walk Score methodology: "It is possible to get on a bus."

Per the Transit Capacity and Quality of Service Manual (TCQSM, TCRP Report 165), 78% of Chilliwack's routes operate at Level of Service D or worse (headways exceeding 20 minutes). The system-wide median headway is 60 minutes. The best-performing route achieves LOS C. TCQSM classifies headways over 30 minutes as 'unattractive to choice riders' (LOS D) and over 60 minutes as 'unattractive to all riders' (LOS F).

Coverage is the primary deficit: 79% of locations within city limits (3,677 of 4,678 grid points) are transit deserts with no bus stop within 800m. Only 1,001 locations have any transit access. Of reachable origin-destination pairs, only 4.0% can be completed by transit within 90 minutes.

Effective speed averages 6.8 km/h, marginally faster than walking. 0% of trips are slower than walking. Mean trip: 52 minutes.

Using the Transport for London PTAL methodology, 99% of grid points score PTAL grade 1a or 1b ('very poor' to 'extremely poor' accessibility). PTAL measures the density of transit service accessible on foot — a low grade indicates few routes, long headways, or long walks to stops.

Travel times are relatively consistent (CV = 0.14), varying ~14% by departure time.

Methodology

science

How this score is computed

The TQI analyzes every origin-destination pair across a 4,678-point grid covering Chilliwack's municipal boundary at 250m spacing. For each pair, at each of 64 departure times (every 15 minutes from 6 AM to 10 PM on a typical Wednesday), the RAPTOR algorithm finds the fastest transit trip including walking to/from stops, wait time, in-vehicle time, and up to 2 transfers.

The Transit Speed Ratio (TSR) measures effective door-to-door speed: straight-line distance divided by total transit time. The Coverage Score (0–100) is the fraction of OD pairs reachable at all. The Speed Score (0–100) normalizes mean TSR between walking speed (5 km/h = 0) and driving speed (40 km/h = 100). The TQI is 50% coverage + 50% speed.

Parameters: max walk to stop 800m, max transfer walk 400m, max trip 90 min, max 2 transfers, walk speed 5 km/h. Data: BC Transit GTFS (Operator 13, Fraser Valley Region), filtered to Chilliwack routes. Municipal boundary: City of Chilliwack Open Data.

Standards & Sources

Established frameworks referenced in this report

Walk Score Transit Score — 0–100 transit accessibility scale developed by Walk Score (Redfin). Uses GTFS data to measure transit service quality. Categories: Minimal Transit (0–24), Some Transit (25–49), Good Transit (50–69), Excellent Transit (70–89), Rider's Paradise (90–100). walkscore.com/transit-score-methodology.shtml

TCQSM Level of Service — Transit Capacity and Quality of Service Manual, 3rd Edition (TCRP Report 165, Transportation Research Board, 2013). Grades transit routes A–F based on service frequency, with LOS C (15–20 min headway) as the maximum desirable wait and LOS D+ as unattractive to choice riders.

PTAL — Public Transport Accessibility Level. Developed by London Borough of Hammersmith & Fulham (1992), adopted as standard by Transport for London. Measures accessibility based on walk time to stops and service frequency using Equivalent Doorstep Frequency (EDF). Grades 1a (extremely poor) to 6b (excellent). Used internationally in London, Australia, India, and Greater Manchester.

RAPTOR Algorithm — Delling, D., Pajor, T., & Werneck, R. (2012). "Round-Based Public Transit Routing." ALENEX. The routing engine used to compute transit travel times.
